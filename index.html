Exploiting TF-Conformers for
General Music Enhancement

Abstract

With the rise of video platforms on the internet, such as YouTube, there are many musical recordings of live performances captured by mobile devices that are highly vulnerable to being distorted with noise and reverberation. As a result, the demand for music enhancement, the process of turning distorted musical recordings into clean studio-quality music, has increased to improve the listening experience. In light of this, we suggest a music enhancement system based on the conformer, which has shown outstanding performance in speech enhancement tasks. We explore the self-attention techniques of the conformer and examine their performance to discover the best approach for the music enhancement task. Our experimental results show that the proposed model outperforms the previous state-of-the-art model when enhancing single-stem music. Furthermore, our system can perform general music enhancement with multi-track mixtures, which was not examined in previous works.
